Permoria is a platform dedicated to the creation, documentation, and dissemination of artistic interventions in public space, understanding these actions as practices capable of transforming the original meaning of a place or an artistic object. Each intervention alters the context, introduces a new reading, and makes visible a layer of thought that, without that action, would remain hidden.

In this ongoing dialogue between memory / territory / action, Permoria builds a living archive in which interventions cease to be mere marks and become narratives: gestures that reformulate collective perception and rewrite the spaces we inhabit.

The visual identity draws on that idea of layers, traces, and modifications, creating a system that functions both as register and as map — a way of understanding how each intervention alters what we see, what we remember, and what we think about public space.
